# Question

Given functions $f_1$, $f_2$, $g_1$, $g_2$ such that $f_1(n) = O(g_1(n))$ and $f_2(n) = O(g_2(n))$, it follows that $f_1(n) \cdot f_2(n) = O(g_1(n) \cdot g_2(n))$.

# Answer

True

# Explanation

By definition, there exist constants $c_1, c_2 > 0$ such that $f_1(n) \leq c_1 \cdot g_1(n)$ and $f_2(n) \leq c_2 \cdot g_2(n)$ for sufficiently large $n$. Multiplying: $f_1(n) \cdot f_2(n) \leq (c_1 \cdot c_2) \cdot g_1(n) \cdot g_2(n)$. So $f_1 \cdot f_2 = O(g_1 \cdot g_2)$.
